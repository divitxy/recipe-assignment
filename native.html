Native Rice

Nigerian Native Rice, often referred to as "Ofada Rice" or "Basmati Rice" in some regions, is a traditional variety of rice that holds a special place in Nigerian cuisine. It is known for its unique, slightly nutty flavor and firm texture, which makes it distinct from other rice varieties. Native rice is typically grown in specific regions of Nigeria and is often used in special dishes like Jollof Rice and various stews. It pairs well with a wide range of traditional Nigerian dishes, including spicy meat and vegetable stews. Its popularity is due to its rich taste and the cultural significance it holds in Nigerian culinary traditions.
