Beans (Ewa Riro)

Ewa Riro is a traditional Nigerian dish made from black-eyed beans, known for its rich and hearty flavor. The name "Ewa Riro" translates to "stirred beans" in Yoruba, reflecting the method of cooking where beans are simmered with a flavorful spicy tomato-based sauce. The dish is known for its rich, savory taste and hearty texture. The sauce typically includes ingredients like tomatoes, onions, peppers, and various seasonings, creating a robust and aromatic flavor profile. Ewa Riro is commonly enjoyed with a side of rice, plantains, or garri (cassava flour), making it a versatile and satisfying meal. This dish is a staple in Nigerian cuisine and is cherished for its comforting, home-cooked feel and its ability to bring people together around the table.

prep time

10 mins.

cook time

1hr

serving

10 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Appetizers

instructions

step 1

Pick out the debris in the Beans. Then rinse well and drain.

step 2

Put the beans inside the pot together with the bell pepper, habanero, onion, and smoked turkey wings. add water and leave to come to a rolling boil over high heat.

step 3

Reduce the heat to medium-low and continue to cook until the beans become tender. about one hour. a little more or less.

step 4

Remove the bell pepper, habanero, and onion Blend and add to the beans.

step 5

Remove the smoked turkey, debone, cut the meat into small pieces, and put it back inside the beans.

step 6

Add the salt, seasoning powder (seasoning cube), crayfish, and palm oil. Add water if necessary and stir well until everything is well combined.

step 7

Leave to simmer for about 3 to 5 minutes and serve with Plantains, Bread, or Garri. Enjoy!

ingredients

3 cups black-eyed peas

1 Red Bell Pepper

1 medium onion

1 roasted Turkey wing

1 habanero pepper

1 tablespoon crayfish

1/2 tablespoon seasoning powder

1/4 cup palm oil or to taste

6 to 9 cups water

salt to taste

Pot

Knife and Cutting Board

Blender or Food Processor

Cooking Spoon

Measuring Cups and Spoons
