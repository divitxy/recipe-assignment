Yam Porridge

Savor the Comfort: Master the Art of Nigerian Yam Porridge!

Yam porridge/Yam pottage

Nigerian Yam Porridge, also known as Yam Pottage or "Asaro" in Yoruba, is a hearty and comforting dish made from yam, a starchy tuber that is a staple in Nigerian cuisine. The dish is prepared by cooking yam chunks in a savory blend of tomatoes, onions, palm oil, and spices until the yams are tender and partially mashed, creating a thick, flavorful porridge. It often includes additional ingredients like vegetables, dried fish, or crayfish to enhance the flavor. Yam Porridge is a popular meal across Nigeria, enjoyed for its rich taste and satisfying texture, making it a favorite for both everyday meals and special occasions.

prep time

10 mins.

cook time

25 mins.

serving

4 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Appetizers

instructions

step 1

Peel and cut the Yam into medium-sized chunks. Rinse a couple of times until the water is no longer cloudy. Set aside.

step 2

Blend the bell pepper, habanero, tomato, and half onion.

step 3

Put the yams inside a pot, and add the blended sauce, salt, bouillon powder, and water. Cover and let it cook for 10 minutes on medium to high heat.

step 4

Stir in the palm oil, sliced onions, and crayfish, cover, and leave to cook for an additional 10 minutes.

step 5

Mash the yams with a potato masher or a wooden spoon. I love to mash some yams and keep some chunks. It gives more texture!

step 6

Add the fish (You can use whatever addition you prefer, like smoked fish or crab meat).

step 7

Allow it to simmer for an additional 3 to 5 minutes.

step 8

Stir in the herbs like basil, or leafy greens like spinach, kale, or ugwu and enjoy while still hot!

ingredients

1 medium Yam tuber

1 Red Bell Pepper

1 large Tomato

1 Habanero Pepper

1 large Onion divided into two

3 cups Water

1/2 Cup Palm Oil of fresh

2 Tablespoons Crayfish

Salt to taste

2 tsp chicken bouillon powder or 2 cubes

15 ounces Mackerel fish salmon,
stockfish or dried fish

Basil or parsley or any Leafy vegetables
(spinach, kale, ugwu)

tools

Pot

Knife and Cutting Board

Wooden Spoon or Cooking Spoon

Measuring Cups and Spoons

Bowl

Grater (optional)

Sieve or Colander
