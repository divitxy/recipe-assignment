Grill the Thrill: Master the Art of Nigerian Suya!

Suya

Nigerian Suya is a popular street food and barbecue delicacy made from thinly sliced meat, typically beef, chicken, or goat, that is marinated in a spicy peanut-based seasoning known as "yaji." The meat is threaded onto skewers and grilled over an open flame, giving it a smoky, charred flavor. Suya is often served with sliced onions, tomatoes, and sometimes cabbage, and is enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or main dish. Known for its bold, spicy taste and irresistible aroma, Suya is a beloved part of Nigerian culinary culture, enjoyed at gatherings, parties, and as a delicious street-side treat.

prep time

20 mins.

cook time

25 mins.

serving

22 servings

Tags : Breakfast Lunch Dinner Appetizers

instructions

step 1

Prep the skewers: Soak the skewers in water for at least 30 minutes before using them.

step 2

Prep the steak: Slice the meat into about 1/8-1/4 thin slices. Then cut into roughly 2-inch pieces.

step 3

Season: Season the beef with salt and bouillon powder (or cube). Add the oil and mix well.

step 4

Marinate: Cover with a tight-fitting lid or plastic wrap. Then leave it in the refrigerator for at least an hour to marinate.

step 5

Thread the beef onto the skewers—about 4 to 5 pieces per skewer.

step 6

Coat with the suya spice (yaji).

step 7

Cook at 400F for 20 minutes, flipping halfway through the cooking process.

step 8

Serve: Remove from skewers and serve with freshly sliced tomatoes, onions, and cucumbers.

ingredients

2 ½ lb beef Flank steak, skirt steak, sirloin

½ to 1 teaspoon salt

1/2 teaspoon beef bouillon or chicken
[or use the cube]

cayenne pepper optional

3/4 cup suya spice plus more for dusting

1 tablespoon peanut oil or any other oil of choice

tools

Grill or Charcoal Grill

Skewers

Mixing Bowl

Knife and Cutting Board

Basting Brush

Measuring Spoons
